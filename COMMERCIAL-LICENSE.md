## When you might need a commercial license

The AGPL's copyleft requires that if you modify demiplane and make it available
to others over a network, you offer those users the corresponding source of your
modified version under the AGPL. Some organizations cannot accept those terms —
for example:

- Embedding or redistributing demiplane inside a **proprietary product** without
  releasing your modifications under the AGPL.
- Offering demiplane (or a derivative) as a **hosted/SaaS service** without
  meeting the AGPL's network-source obligations.
- Internal **policies that prohibit AGPL-licensed software**.

For these cases, a **commercial license** is available. It grants the right to
use demiplane without the AGPL's copyleft obligations, under negotiated terms.

This dual-licensing model — open source for everyone, commercial terms for those
who need them — is what funds continued development. It is made possible by the
contributor [CLA](./CLA.md).
